# Product Charter

## Target Users

- Individuals who maintain private PDF libraries.
- Invite-only beta users who need citation-backed answers over their own documents.
- Future administrators who need controlled user management, review, and auditability.

## Problem Statement

Private knowledge collections are difficult to search, summarize, and trust. Users need a system that can answer questions from evidence, preserve useful context, and keep ownership and access control explicit.

## Product Promise

ContextOS helps users work with private documents through grounded answers, controlled memory, and transparent governance. The system prioritizes trust, evidence, and user control over maximum automation.

## Beta Model

The beta is free, invite-only, publicly hosted, and limited to three users. It is intended to validate the product with a small trusted cohort before broader release decisions.

## Core Product Loops

### Secure Entry

Users enter through verified identity and explicit invitation controls, with ownership and access enforced before data is exposed.

### Document Knowledge

Users upload supported PDFs, store them privately, and retrieve evidence from their own document library with citations.

### Grounded Conversation

Users ask questions in persistent conversations that stay tied to document evidence, summaries, and current context.

### Controlled Memory

The system can suggest memory candidates, but only approved memories may influence future answers.

### User and Administrator Control

Users can review, export, and delete their data, while administrators use explicit, audited controls for invitation and account management.

## Success Principles

- Trust is more important than broad automation.
- Users should understand where answers came from.
- Ownership must be deterministic and enforceable.
- Deletion must actually remove access and derived visibility.
- Memory should be helpful only after approval.
- Security and transparency should remain visible in the product, not hidden behind the interface.

## Portfolio Purpose

ContextOS should demonstrate the ability to design and document a security-aware, multi-tenant knowledge system with strong ownership controls, grounded AI behavior, and a clear product boundary.

## Trust and Control

Trust and control matter more than maximum automation. The product is intentionally constrained so users remain able to govern access, memory, and deletion decisions.
